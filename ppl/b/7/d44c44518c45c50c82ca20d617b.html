Narrative

Story

Connecting Context: From Clark, William Henry (1809)
Grave for William Henry includes his parent John Clark (d. 10-11-1848, aged 60, so b. ~1788) and Clark, Elizabeth (d. 20-4-1849, aged 59, so b. ~1789). William Henry Clark's death record indicates he was from Kent, England. The only family with a William Henry, Richard, and John Clark, born in Kent in the right period, is the family from the Snodland area.

John Clark's parents William Clerk and Sarah How, were married in Snodland on 6-6-1779. John was born in early 1786, the 5th of at least 8 children, and was baptised in All Saints Church, Snodland, on 19th March 1786 [Note 1].

John's wife, Elizabeth Jenkins [Note 2] was born in 1787 in nearby East Malling. John and Elizabeth were married in Wateringbury (near East Malling) on 2-3-1806, and their first child John jnr was born 4 months later in East Malling. The family then moved back to Snodland, where Elizabeth (b. 1808, d. 1808) and then William were born. The family then appears to have moved to nearby Halling [Note 3] where Mary Ann (b. 1814, d 1816), Richard (1816), and George (1818) were born. Another possible son, Thomas, might also have been born at some point in this period [Note 4].

Sometime between 1818 and 1835 the family (parents and at least four, and possibly five, sons) appears to have emigrated to NSW, though the records for this emigration have yet to be located, and it is not known whether they arrived together or separately, nor even definitively confirmed that John and Elizabeth emigrated to Australia [Note 5]. This move might have been triggered by Elizabeth's brother, John Jenkins being convicted, in 1820 in the Kent Assizes, for Burglary and (after originally being sentenced to be hanged) transported to NSW, arriving in May 1821. John's wife and children (i.e. William's Aunt and cousins) arrived in the colony in Jan 1827, so this might date John's family immigration.

John Jenkin (and his family when they arrived in 1827) were living in Sutton Forest, and John's family may have travelled first to there. By the mid 1830's the family was likely further south as John's son John jnr was married in Camden Forest (now Holbrook) in 1835. On 22nd July 1839 John's son William married the 15 year old Elizabeth Harris in Bowning, NSW. There are also various references to the family being in the Yass area during this time (see the information for son William, in particular). At the end of the 1830's at least three sons - William, Richard, and John - moved south into Victoria and establish squatter runs, river punts and hotels in the areas that were to subsequently become Wangaratta, Benalla, and Violet Town.

No further details can be found for John and Elizabeth, and no death record, probate, or newspaper obituary could be located. There are some newspaper references to John and Elizabeth Clarke in Sydney in 1835 and in April 1839 there is reference to a John and Elizabeth Clarke departing Sydney aboard the Louisa, heading to Port Philip, so it is possible they travelled to Melbourne via ship whilst their sons travelled inland (with stock and equipment), though this is only speculation. Irrespective of how they travelled south, it is likely that John and Elizabeth ended up in Wangaratta at some point, as a gravestone exists in Wangaratta for them both - showing John dying on November 10th 1848, aged 60 years, and his wife Elizabeth dying less than 6 months later on April 20th 1849 aged 59 years. Their sons went on to become very successful pioneers in northern Victoria.

Note 1:
The spelling of John's surname varies significantly. The marriage of John's parents list "William Clerk / Sarah How". John's baptism lists "John, son of Wm and Sarah Clark". John's marriage lists ""John Clarke / Elizabeth Jinkins". The baptism of his first son lists "John Clarke". The records in Snodland (baptism for William and Elizabeth) both list "Clerk". The record in Halling (baptisms for Mary Ann, Richard and George) list a mix of "Clerk" and "Clark".

Once in Australia, the variations continue. As noted in https://conversationswithgrandma.wordpress.com/2014/03/31/sepia-saturday-the-punt-on-the-river-and-other-tales/, "In most of these works William Henry Clark, Richard Clark and John Clark are recorded as Clarke. This is due to early maps which often incorrectly recorded their surname with an E at the end. I have seen many signatures of these men and they were quite consistent in spelling their name without an E".

Note 2:
On the marriage records Elizabeth's surname is shown as Jinkins.

Note 3:
This is based on the location of the baptisms, though Halling and Snodland are only 2 miles apart, so the family may have just changed their parish without moving? This also happened during the time that Thomas might have been born, and so may be connected to the difficulty in locating details on Thomas - see Note 4 below.

Note 4:
An obituary for Thomas Clark (who died in Wangaratta in 1879) describes him as being a brother of William Henry Clark, and his death index gives his mother as Elizabeth Jenkins. The obituary also states "we should guess his age at about 65", putting his birth around 1810. However no relevant baptism or birth record can be found in Halling or Snodland. A number of internet trees give his birth as 8-4-1810 in Elmstead, but this is too soon after William's birth and Elmstead is a long way from Snodland. It might be possible that his birth was unregistered, or he was an illegitimate son of John. As a possible example, there is a Thomas Wooding, b. 2-3-1806 to Sarah Wooding, no father in East Malling (i.e. same day as marriage of John to Elizabeth in East Malling)! There is no other record for a Sarah Wooding (no marriage) and no other record could be found for Thomas Wooding.

Note 5:
The only record currently located for William and Elizabeth in Australia is the gravestone in Wangaratta. Could this just be a memorial stone (unlikely but possible)?
