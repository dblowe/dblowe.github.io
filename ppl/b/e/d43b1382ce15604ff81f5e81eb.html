Narrative

Story

Irene May Maher (known as ‘May’) was born on 12th Sep 1891 in Numurkah (60km west of Numurkah), the youngest of 5 sons and 5 daughters of Michael Maher and Bridget Roach. Her parents were both born in the very early days of the Victorian colony and had married in Yea in 1868, then moved to Nagambie then Numurkah, before moving to Mt Gwynne (near Mulwala, NSW) sometime after her birth, but likely before 1904 [Note 1] . Her father died around 1918 and the family moved to Yarrawonga.

At age 28, the 1919 electoral roll shows her living in Piper Street, Yarrawonga, and working on “Home Duties” (she may have been living with Thomas Francis Maher, her 42 year old brother, who was also living in Piper Street).

In 1921 she married William Charles Lowe in Wagga Wagga [Note 2], and by Nov 1923 William’s address is shown as “Yam Reham” (“May Maher” spelt backwards), Norman St, Sth Wagga Wagga. During the 1920’s her mother Bridget lived with her in Wagga, up until her death in 1928.

William and May had at least 5 children: Michael John (1922, died as an infant), Irene May (1923), Bridgette Mary (1926), Margaret Bernadette (1928), and John Charles (1934).

From the time of their marriage until her death in 1985 (around 65 years) May lived in Wagga Wagga, except for a few years in Cootamundra. Sometime in the late 1920’s, during the depression, they lost the family house in Norman Street, and moved into a rental house in Fox Street [Note 3]. The 1934 and 1937 rolls show her living back at 5 Norman Street Wagga, so the family must have been able to recover the house. The 1943 electoral roll has her living at 16 Docker Street, where she remained at least until 1968. In 1972 she is living in Cootamundra with her daughter Bridget Mary and in 1980 she was living at 19 Morgan Street, Wagga.

May died on 14th October 1985, aged 94, and is buried in Wagga Wagga Cemetery.

----

Note 1:
There is a reference, in an article on a Wedding in Numurkah on 2nd Dec 1904, to Mr and Mrs M Maher of Mt Gwynne.

Note 2:
Oral recollections from John Lowe (son) indicate that the move to Wagga Wagga was because May was Catholic, whereas William was Church of England, and the families did not approve of the Marriage. May temporarily moved to Temora and worked as a barmaid, whilst William established a business in Wagga. This would be consistent with her sister Margaret and brother-in-law Owen Smith running the Federal Hotel in Temora at the time (see Owen Smith’s obituary).

Note 3:
Oral recollections (John Lowe), supported by the 1930 electoral roll.
