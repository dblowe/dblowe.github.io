Narrative

Story

Connecting Context:
The death record for Elizabeth Duke Weber (nee Rice) gives her parents as William Duke Rice, Retired Master Mariner, and Honoria Pendergast.

Honora Rice, nee Prendergast was born around 1822 near Westport in County Mayo, Ireland, though the specific location is yet to be identified [note 1]. Her parents were Thomas Prendergast and Mysie Kemp, and there are some references to her maternal grandfather having been a "General Alexander Kemp" who fought with Wellington at Waterloo and Honora having known a number of the officers involved in Waterloo [note 2]. It is not known whether Honora had any siblings. It is likely though that she remained in the area around Westport for 30-odd years, as her immigration records (in 1852) indicate that her mother Mysie was still alive and living in Westport.

In 1852 Honora payed Â£1 to migrate to Australia, travelling aboard the ship Hydaspes and arriving in Sydney on 17th December 1852. The passenger records give her name as Honor Prendergast, age 29, occupation as general house servant, religion as Church of Rome, able to read and write, and with no relatives in the colony.

On the 28th Feb 1855, after just over 2 years in the colony, Honora married William Rice [note 3] in the Parish of St James, Sydney, with the service being carried out by Charles F. D. Priddle. William was resident in the Parish of St Phillip (this covers the western side of Sydney, including the Rocks, Wynyard, etc.) and Honora was from the Parish of St James (the eastern side of the city). Over the next 6 years William and Honora had four daughters: Elizabeth Duke Rice (b. 1856-3-4, Broughton Creek - d. 1903-4-11), Mysie Eleanor (b. 1857, Sydney - d. 1939-9-23), Clara Sophie Rice (1859, Sydney - d. 1940-6-27), and Letitia Geraldine Rice (1861, Sydney - 1896-12-20). Elizabeth was the only one of the daughters to marry (to Carl Weber in 1875).

In 1856, shortly after marrying, a newspaper article indicates that William was working as a waterman at Circular Quay in Sydney (Sydney's population at this stage was around 50,000). During the next 40 years William spend much of his time at sea, sailing both along the NSW coast, but also internationally (to the UK multiple times as well as NZ).

At some point in the 1860 or early 1870's William (or Honora) is likely to have bought property around Darkes Forest, north of Wollongong as information on Carl Weber (who married William and Honora's daughter Elizabeth) indicates that whilst working on the initial surveys of the Sydney Water Supply in late 1874 he was camped in a scrubby paddock owned by Honora, who was living at Darkes Forest with her four daughters.

In January 1875 Carl married Elizabeth. It is likely that William was away during this marriage as he only arrived back in Sydney from London, on 29/4/1875 captaining the ship Wallacetown.

By 1890 it was likely that William (and his family) was living in Sylvania, as an archive photo shows William sailing on the Georges River with his dog. According to William's obituary he retired around 1899.

William died on 24th August 1909, aged 84, at Sylvania in Sydney and was buried on 25th Aug 1909 at the Church of England Cemetery, Sutherland (now known as Woronora Cemetery). Honora died four years later on 1st October 1913 and was buried with William.

Note 1:
Honora's death certificate in 1913 gives her age as 91, putting her birth as ~1822. This is consistent with other records, such as her marriage certificate and immigration records. The death certificate also indicates that she was born in "Brazool, Ireland", though such a place cannot be found. An obituary stated that she was "born near Westport in 1822". Westport is on the border of the Baronies of Burrishoole and Murrisk in County Mayo (so the reference to "Brazool" may actually be "Burrishoole"). The immigration passenger records also list her origin. Whilst these are difficult to interpret, they appear to indicate Daigoom/Daigorm/Brigoom.

Note 2:
An obituary for Honora (in the St George Call) stated: "Deceased was the daughter of Mysie and Thomas Prendergast, of Westport, Ireland, and grand-daughter of General Kemp, who distinguished himself at the battle of Waterloo. She was born near Westport in 1822, and came to Sydney in 1852, where she married the late Captain Rice. Mrs. Rice's relationship to several of the officers under Wellington caused her to have an intimate knowledge of the events attending the battle of Waterloo". A second obituary (Illwarra Mercury) stated "Honora, widow of the late William Rice, master mariner, and granddaughter of the late General Alexander Kemp".

Note 3:
On the marriage record Honora is listed as Honoria Pendergrast. This is the only place her name is spelt this way.
