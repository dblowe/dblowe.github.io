Narrative

Story

Connecting context: William Charles Lowe’s birth record indicates that his mother was Margaret Sarah Moore. John Charles Lowe’s marriage record in 1888 gives his wife as Margaret Sarah Moore.

Margaret Sarah Anne Moore was born on 20th August 1868 in Wangaratta, Victoria, the third child of John Moore and Elizabeth Clark. She was baptised in Holy Trinity Church, Wangaratta on Sept 13th 1868. Margaret (along with her older sister Charlotte) is missing from the BMD records, but does appear in the church register and various other family records [Note 1]. Margaret’s four grandparents were all early settlers in the Wangaratta area, with her maternal grandfather being known as the “Father of Wangaratta”, having settled there in the late 1830’s and become a prominent local figure.

Margaret stayed in Wangaratta until her marriage to John Charles Lowe on 8th Nov 1888, again in Holy Trinity Church. The couple settled in Yarrawonga, 50km from Wangaratta, and over the next 20 years from 1889 to 1909 they had at least 9 children, all born in Yarrawonga [Note 2].

Over the next 20 years, JL had 9 children. He worked primarily as a carpenter, despite having only one hand. In 1918 he built the first hospital in Yarrawonga for his two older daughters who were nurses and ran it primarily as a small maternity hospital for only a few years in the early 1920's.

Margaret's husband John became very active in the community. He instigated the formation of the first football club, in which he played, and later umpired. He was also the driving force behind the selection and clearing of land for a football ground, which eventually was named in his honours (and remains the JC Lowe Oval to this day). John and Margaret's oldest son (John aka Jack aka Scotty) was also a very good football player, playing for Collingwood in 1912 and 1913, and for the premiership winning Carlton in 1914.

Margaret died at her home in Yarrawonga on Jan 12th 1920, aged 51 [Note 3]. Her newspaper obituary indicated that she "had not enjoyed good health for some time".

Note 1:
There is a newspaper article describing John Moore (the name of Margaret's father) in gaol in Wangaratta in Oct 1868. This might explain why Margaret's birth was not properly registered? The probate documents for John Moore (in 1899) also mention all the children, including Margaret, in the correct birth order, confirming the family connection.

Note 2:
Birth records can be found for children 2-9, but no record can be found for the possible first child, Elizabeth. She does, however appear in numerous other family trees as the first child of JC Lowe, with a date of birth of 24/2/1889 – so only 3 ½ months after the marriage (which might explain why the birth isn’t registered?). In the son William’s obituary there is a reference to his sister Mrs NC Scott?

Note 3:
Some records (including the death index) have the age incorrectly shown as 53.
