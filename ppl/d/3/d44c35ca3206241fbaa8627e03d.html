Narrative

Story

Connecting Context: Personal history

Mona Geraldine Weber was born on 10th April 1903 at Copeland, NSW, first child of Guido Weber and Eleanor (Nell) Talbot. Her parents had married on 9th July 1902 in Wollongong, and shortly afterwards Guido, who was a teacher, had been transferred to Copeland North School (a small school around 9 miles west of Gloucester, NSW, opened in 1880 and closed in 1912 [Note 1]). Sometime in the next 5 years Guido was transferred to Lostock (on the Paterson Rover, 12 miles above Gresford, west of Dungog) where they remained until at least 1911 [Note 2].

On 4th April 1911, Mona's mother Eleanor died (Eleanor was aged 34, Mona was 1 week from turning 8), and the following year in 1912 Guido married Eleanor's sister (and hence Mona's aunt) Caroline. At some stage in the next 5 years the family moved to Dubbo, with Guido teaching at Delroy school. In 1916 however Guido enlisted to serve in WWI. A newspaper article in October 1916 (when Mona was 13) states:

"Private G Weber, teacher of the Delroy Public School, has been some time in the Dubbo Camp, and had it not been for his contracting measles he would have gone forward last week. However he will be leaving soon, and in view of this fact Mrs Weber and himself and family were the guests of the Delroy Parents' and Citizens' Association … and the three children were also the recipients of several tokens of esteem. … It was admitted by several parents that Mr Weber is the best teacher the school has ever known. ... Mrs Weber and family on Wednesday left for Wollongong, where they will take up their residence pending Mr Weber's return to Civic life."

In 1919, upon Guido's return, he was working as Assistant Principal at Wollongong Public School, but by 1920 the family was again near Newcastle. A 1920 newspaper article describes a class of girls from Newcastle High School, being coached in swimming by Mona. Then at the beginning of 1921 Mona received her School Leaving Certificate - studying English, Latin, Mathematics, and Chemistry (with results published in the newspaper on Jan 31st 1921).

Two years later, in December 1922, Mona was appointed as a Junior teacher to Waratah public school (a suburb of Newcastle). During 1923 Guido and Caroline (and Mona's younger siblings) moved south again, with Guido as headmaster at Tomerong School near Nowra. At some time in the following few years Mona moved to Tuggerah Entrance School (opened in 1915 and still operating at the same location).

On 30th April 1925 she resigned from the public service and two days later, on 2/5/1925, at the age of 22, she married George Preston (who was aged 32). At some point in the next few years (likely around 1928 [Note 3]) George and Mona moved to 10 King Street, Stockton where they lived for the rest of their lives. They then had three children: Douglas (26/7/1926), Maxwell (17/9/1930), and Joan (12/10/1934). George became a well-respected member of the local community - particularly with regard to fishing and the surf clubs, and both Mona and George were very active in the local community.

By 1942 Mona was secretary of the CUSA (Catholic Unites Services Association).

George died on Saturday 27/11/1948 aged 55, and was buried at Stockton, and early the following year (with 3 children, the youngest aged 14) Mona began teaching at Stockton school.

Returning to teaching, and the loss of George, did not slow down Mona's civic activity. In December 1949, already being President of the local branch of the Legion of Catholic Women, the Bishop of Maitland appointed Mona president of the Diocesan Council of the Legion of Catholic Women. A newspaper article from 1951 indicates that she was still president.

Mona remained living in Stockton, and died on 30th January 1985, aged 81, 36 years after her husband George. She was buried in Stockton cemetery.

Note 1:
The transfer comes from Govt Gazette records. For details of the school see http://search.records.nsw.gov.au/agencies/3107.

Note 2:
Family history notes from Daphne Salt indicate that they were in Lostock in 1907, and Govt Gazette records show them there in 1911.

Note 3:
George's obituary (1948) indicates that "He was a foundation member of Newcastle Surf Club, and retained his membership for 20 years till he moved to Stockton about 15 years ago". Newcastle Surf Club was founded in 1908. This places the move to Stockton between 1928 and 1933. A newspaper article from 1930 however indicated he was elected "Club Captain" of Stockton Surf Club in 1930, so the move to Stockton was prior to 1930.
