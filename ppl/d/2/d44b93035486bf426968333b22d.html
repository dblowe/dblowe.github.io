Narrative

Story

Note: Records for Michael Maher need to be interpreted carefully as there appear to be a number of different people with the name Michael Maher in Victoria in the mid-1800’s .

Michael parents, John Maher and Margaret Whelan, arrived in the Victorian Colony in late 1841 along with their 6 year old son Patrick. In April 1842 they had another son, John, born in Melbourne. Michael was then born in Geelong on Jan 12th 1843, and baptised on Jan 14th 1843 X1,R1].

Melbourne was founded (by Batman) in August 1835, and Geelong was surveyed 3 weeks later, and then gazetted as a town in October 1838 with a population of 545 (only 3 years before John and Margaret arrived, and 4 years 3 months before Michael was born). The first Catholic church in Geelong was opened on 27th Nov 1842 – just 7 weeks before Michael was born [Note 2].

John and Margaret had a number of additional children that indicated they remained around (or returned to) Geelong until the late 1850’s: Martin (4/8/1844, Barrabool Hills), Edward (12/9/1846, Mt Eliza), William (15/6/1848, Collingwood), Elizabeth (1851), and Anne (30/11/1857, Duck Ponds - now known as Lara). There may have been another child in the late 1850’s – Mary Anne – though this may have just been Anne using a different name.

There is no further record of Michael until 1868 (the Victorian gold rush occurred in the 1850’s, and whilst he would only have been 7 years old when gold was discovered, his family may have visited the gold fields. Michael was 11 years old at the time of the Eureka rebellion).

On the 23rd February 1968, aged 25, Michael married Bridget Roche (later spelt Roach) at Hughes Creek. Michael is listed as a farmer living at Tabilk, whilst Bridget is listed as having been born at Yea and currently also living at Tabilk. The marriage was celebrated by Fr Brannigan (the third Priest in the parish of Kilmore – 60km South of Tabilk [Note 3]), who also celebrated another marriage on the same day and location.

Bridget’s obituary stated “Mrs. Maher was born on the farm of her father, the late Mr. John Roche, in the Strathbogie Ranges, where the town of Yea now stands. She married when 20 years old Michael Maher, of Yea. They afterwards lived variously at Nagambie and Numurkah and then came to Mt. Gwynne, near Mulwala, NSW.”

Parish Plans for the Nagambie area (around 120km north of Melbourne) – specifically the Parish of Tabilk and the Parish of Wormangal – show a number of “selections” by Michael, as well as the family of his wife Bridget, during the early 1870’s.

Michael and Bridget subsequently had at least 12 children. Whilst two died as infants, the rest of the children – 5 sons and 5 daughters – all outlived Michael. The first 7 were born in Avanel/Nagambie: Margaret (1869), John (1870), Mary (1872), Martin (1874), Louis (1876), Thomas (1877) and William (1880).

The next child – Catherine (1882) – was born in Tatura, 50 km north of Nagambie. The family then moved another 50km north to Numurkah, where the last 4 children were born: Ellen (1884), Annie (1887), Isidore (1889) and Irene May (1891).

At some point between 1891 and 1904 [Note 4] the family moved another 70km north to Mt Gwynne, just across the border into NSW.

Michael died on 28th Apr 1919 in Piper Street [Note 5], Yarrawonga (though there is some confusion over the year) [Note 6]. The death is reported as due to “heart failure”. His wife’s subsequent obituary, 8 years later, stated:

"After Mr. Maher's death, about eight years ago, his widow went to Yarrawonga, and later to Wagga, and lived with her daughters, Mrs. James Hanrahan [i.e. Mary] and Mrs W. Lowe [i.e. Irene May]. Mrs. Maher reared a family of five son's and five daughters, all of whom are living. The sons were present at the funeral. About 50 grandchildren and 20 great-grandchildren are included in those who mourn their loss. Three of deceased's sons are at Yarrawonga, one at Daysdale, and one (Bill) is among the soldier settlers at Griffith, N.S.W."
