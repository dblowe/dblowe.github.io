Narrative

Story

Jacob Preston was born in Townbent, Oswaldtwistle in Jun 1795, and baptised in St James Church in Church Kirk, the Deanery of Accrington on 16 Jul 1795, as the natural son of Ann Preston [Note 1]. He seems to have spent his whole life living in and around Accrington, Lancashire.

On 20th Apr 1830, aged 34, he married Mary Bridge, aged 24, in St James church, Accrington (Jacob signed the register but Mary only provided a ‘X’). On 3rd May 1832 a daughter was born, Mary Ann Preston – and on 23rd Sept 1832 she was christened in the Wesleyan Church, Accrington [Notes 2,3]. There is, however, no further mention of his wife Mary after this time - so she may have died in childbirth? The daughter, Mary Ann, might have gone to live with relatives of the deceased wife, as she is not recorded in the 1841 census with Jacob. She does however appear in the 1851 census as an 18 year old lodger. She then married Thomas Fowler sometime around 1861. Interestingly, she was re-baptised in an Anglican church in 1890, as a 57 year old widow.

On 2nd, 9th and 16th Nov 1834 banns were posted for Jacob Preston and Sarah Tyler in the parish of Burnley (about 7 miles from Accrington), though they appear to not have married. Jacob and Sarah’s first son William was born 6 months later in Apr/May 1835. Banns were again posted in Aug/Sep 1836, with Jacob being shown as a Calico Printer. Jacob and Sarah were then married on 5 Sep 1836 [Note 4].

Jacob and his second wife Sarah went on to have at least 5 more sons (John in 1837, Henry in 1839, James in 1840, Thomas in 1842 and John in 1846) with the second John being the only one to have survived past childhood. In the baptism records Jacob’s occupation is typically shown as “Labourer” and he was living in Chapel Street, then Blackburn Road, and then Brick House.

The 1841 census shows Jacob (45), his second wife Sarah (37) and William (6) living in Accrington (though no sign of the older daughter Mary Ann). The 1851 census shows Jacob (55), Sarah (47), William (16), and John (5) still living in New Accrington. The 1861 census shows Jacob (67), Sarah (54), and John (14) still in Accrington (William has moved to Batley with his new wife).

In 1859 Jacob’s daughter from his first marriage, Mary Ann, was married, and the record shows her father Jacob as being a “Furnace Man”. Jacob’s second wife Sarah died in 1865. The only Jacob Preston listed who is listed deceased between 1850 and 1870 in Lancashire was on 28 May 1867, in Accrington, aged 71.

--

Note 1:
1841, 1851 and 1861 census records have Jacob born in Oswaldtwistle between 1794 and 1796. The christening index [X1] shows Jacob baptized on 16/7/1795 in St James, Church Kirk (note that ‘Church’ is a location – see [W1] below). This is the only relevant birth/christening record for a Jacob Preston born in Lancashire in 1795 +/- 20 years.

Note 2:
Copy of the register entry obtained from http://www.bmdregisters.co.uk/

Note 3:
Jacob’s other children were christened in an Anglican church, so it may be that whoever was looking after Mary Ann (maybe after Mary died) was Wesleyan?

Note 4:
The Jacob Preston for both marriages is likely to be the same, as there is no record of any other Jacob Preston being born in Lancashire between 1775 and 1815).
