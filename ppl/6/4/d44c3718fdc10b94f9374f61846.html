Narrative

Story

William was born around Apr/May 1835 in Accrington, Lancashire [Note 1] to Jacob and Mary Preston [Note 2]. His parents applied for Banns in late 1834 but do not appear to have married. They applied for Banns again in 1836 and married this time (which might be why a birth registration cannot be found for William). The 1841 census shows Willliam (aged 6yo) living with his parents in Accrington, and again in 1851 (aged 16yo), this time working as a Weaver and with a younger brother John.

In 1853 (aged around 18) he joined the Lancashire Constabulary and then rejoined in 1856. On 4th Feb 1857, aged 21 9/12, he was employed by the West Yorkshire constabulary. He is described as 5'10.5", fresh complexion, grey eyes, brown hair, and having been born in Accrington. He could read and write, and was single. It shows that he was previously employed by the Lancashire constabulary for 1 2/12 years. From 2 Mar 1857 he was attached to East Morley division. He transferred to Dewsbury on 22 Oct 1857 and then to L.S. & Tickhill (?) on 16 Apr 1860. In August 1860 he was advanced from 3rd grade to 2nd grade constable, but reverted soon after for misconduct. On 20th Jan 1861 he resigned on account of ill health.

Whilst still stationed at Dewsbury, aged 23, he married Sarah Gray in early 1859, with the marriage registered in Dewsbury. Sarah had a previous child 4 years earlier (William, with no father recorded) though this child remained living with Sarah’s parents.

William and Sarah’s first child John was born around 1860 (he was aged 1 at the April 1861 census), and Frederick was born in 1861, with at least 6 more siblings born over the next 17 years [Note 3]. (The last child, George, was born several months after William died. It is also interesting to note that all the children except John were baptised at the same time (17/11/1877 - 7 months after William died).

In the 1861 census (7/4/1861) William and Sarah are married but not residing at the same address. Sarah is living with her parents (and her children John and Frederick), whilst William is lodging nearby with another family (and employed as a Watchman). He had left the constabulary only 10 weeks earlier due to ill health, so it may be possible he was recovering.

Two years later, on 8th Jun 1863, aged 28 2/12, William was again employed by the West Yorkshire constabulary. He is described as having a broken middle finger on his left hand and a scar on his right calf, living in Batley, married and with two children, and last employed by Mr Isaac Corbett in Batley. He was initially assigned to Saddleworth. On 1 Oct 1866 he was moved to W. Morley, and then on 1st Nov 1871 he was transferred to Upper Agbrigg - a district to the SE of Huddersfield.

Based on the birth locations of his children (shown in the 2/4/1871 and 3/4/1881 censuses) he may have lived in Batley in 1860 (John), Saddleworth or Batley in 1861 (Fred), Sowerby Bridge in 1867 (Ann), Stainland [Note 4] in Feb 1871 (Arthur), Colne Bridge in 1873 (Margaret), and High Burton (Mary) in 1876.

In the 1871 census he is shown living in Lower Green, Stainland with children John (11), Fred (9), Ann (4) and Arthur (2 months) (6 year old Emma can’t be found anywhere in the 1871 census, but does appear in the 1881 census).

On 10 Oct 1869 he was badly assaulted when on duty, and then again on 29 Dec 1872 he was assaulted by a number of drunks. On 3rd April 1877 he resigned through ill health (aged 42). William died very soon afterwards and was buried in Highburton on 14th April 1877 – 11 days after resigning [Note 5]. His last child George was born back in Upper Batley, in late 1877 (i.e. after his death, and when his wife and children had moved back to her home town of Batley).

---

Note 1:
Date and location of birth confirmed by Yorkshire Constable record, but no birth record can be found.

Note 2:
This is the only family with a William Preston born in Accrington in the right period.

Note 3:
The birth location comes from the 1861, 1871 and 1881 censuses for either or both parents (though the birth location varied in some cases, and especially for Frederick).

Note 4:
There is a difference in the birth location of Arthur Preston between the 1871 (Stainland) and 1881 (Holleywell Green) censuses – though the two locations are less than a mile apart.

Note 5:
This is the only William Preston who died in the West Riding of Yorkshire between 1877 and 1881, and approximately the right age.
