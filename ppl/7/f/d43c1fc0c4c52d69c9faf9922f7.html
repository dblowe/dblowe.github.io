Narrative

Story

John Charles Lowe [Notes 1,2] was born on Black Dog Creek, near Chiltern, on Nov 14 1861. His father was a Scottish immigrant (from Redgorton in Perthshire) who arrived during the 1850's goldrush. His mother was an Irish immigrant who arrived in 1855 as an 18yo, following her convict father who had been sent to Van Dieman's Land via Bermuda.

A year after his birth his family moved to Docker's Plains (as selectors), but then sold out and moved to Wangaratta. In his biography he stated that during his school years:

"at every chance I got I would sneak away to a black's camp situated a little below the present show grounds on the river bank and put in many a day in bark canoe on the river and many a time eat roasted possum" [Personal Diary].

At around 10yo his family moved to Benalla. He then finished school at 12yo and went to work in a store, but soon after he lost a hand in a shooting accident, and so went back to school. After failing to get a clerical job or a positon as a pupil teacher, he had various odd jobs - often with his maternal grandfather. In 1876 his grandfather died and left him a horse and dray, which he then used to obtain various jobs [Personal diary].

In 1880, as an 18yo, JL was employed to cart metal from Glenrowan to Benalla for the main street. As a result he became the first person taken hostage by the Kelly gang during the Glenrowan siege. When he died 70 years later he is believed to have been the last person alive to have witnessed the events at Glenrowan.

The following year his father moved to Jingellic to work in a tin mine, and shortly after his mother and younger siblings followed, whilst JL remained in Benalla. Over the next few years he moved around: Norvanan station in NSW (shooting kangaroo), then Benalla, then Jingellic (engine driving on a tin mine), Mount Alfred in NSW (mining), Arara (mining), Yarra Yarra (groom), Wagga Wagga. At this time time his parents had moved to Wagga Wagga as well. He then moved to Yarra in March 1884 to collect a race horse he owned, but the horse was injured so he ended up staying in Yarra for a year working as a groom.

By 1888 JL was back in Yarrawonga, eventually brining his parents and two brothers and two sisters with him. Later in 1888 he married Margaret (Maggie) Moore in Holy Trinity Cathedral, Wangaratta. Maggie was the daughter and granddaughter of local pioneers - her maternal grandfather is referred to as the "Father of Wangaratta" after he was one of the original settlers in the area in 1839, and her maternal grandmother followed her convict father to the colony in the 1830's.

Over the next 20 years, JL had 9 children. He worked primarily as a carpenter, despite having only one hand. In 1918 he built the first hospital in Yarrawonga for his two older daughters who were nurses and ran it primarily as a small maternity hospital for only a few years in the early 1920's.

John also became very active in the community. He instigated the formation of the first football club, in which he played, and later umpired. He was also the driving force behind the selection and clearing of land for a football ground, which eventually was named in his honours (and remains the JC Lowe Oval to this day). In 1932 he was made the second life member of the club (after his son Tom was made the first a year earlier). His oldest son was also a very good football player, playing for Collingwood in 1912 and 1913, and for Carlton in 1914 (including winning the premiership).

John was also the secretary of the local racing club and an active member of the Yarrawonga Fire Brigade, and was a trustee of Grove Park and Grove Pictures.

His wife predeceased him in 1920 aged 53, and he died at the house of his son Albert on 27th October 1950 [Note 3], thirty years after his wife, at the age of 88.

Note 3:
