Narrative

Story

Frederick Preston was born in Batley, Yorkshire in early 1861 [Note 1] to William Preston and Sarah Preston (nee Gray). He was living with his mother and older brother, in his maternal grandparents house, along with several aunts/uncles. His father was recovering from ill health nearby.

His father had resigned in Jan 1861 as a Constable (due to ill health) and moved back to Batley. Two years later his father rejoined the Constabulary, and for the first 16 years of his life he moved extensively around the West Riding of Yorkshire, as his father changed location (at least Saddleworth, Sowerby Bridge, Stainland, Colnebridge, and Highburton [Note 2]).

The 1871 census shows Frederick, aged 9, living in Tower Green, Stainland, West Yorkshire with his parents and three siblings. His birth location is (possibly incorrectly) shown as Saddleworth [Note 3].

Frederick's father resigned from the constabulary in 1877 and died shortly after, and the family returned to Batley, where Frederick's youngest sibling George was born in 1878. (George's daughter Gladys married Maurice Messenger, who remained in contact with Frederick’s family after he migrated to Australia).

Fred was still in Batley for the 1881 census, being shown as aged 20, living with his mother and 6 younger siblings. His occupation is shown as cloth finisher, and he is shown as being born in Batley.

He emmigrated to Australia shortly afterwards - aboard the S.S.Abergeldie, arriving in Sydney on 3rd Feb 1884 [Note 4]. The ships records show him as an Agricultural Labourer from Yorkshire, being C.of E. and being able to read and write. The Immigration record shows the same information, but also having paid £4 for passage, and having no relatives in the colony.

Sometime in the next year Fred married Catherine (Kate) Cooley (Kate’s obituary indicates she was married in Sydney) and then moved to Newcastle where they remained for the rest of their life [Note 5]. Over the next 20 years they had at least 10 children (from birth records), with 3 dying as children, 1 being killed in WW1, and 6 marrying.

In 1889 Fred was working for the Railway - having been a successful candidate for NSW branch of the Railway Ambulance Corps.

On 23 July 1913 he was working as a "leading porter" for the Railway in Newcastle when he died:

"Frederick Preston, a loading porter in the railway service, dropped dead while at work on Leo Wharf, on Wednesday afternoon. He was lashing a truck of timber, when he fell. Dr. Ferguson, who was sent for, pronounced him extinct. The deceased lived In Nobbys road, Newcastle". From The Maitland Daily Mercury (NSW : 1894 - 1939) Thursday 24 July 1913

He was buried on 25/7/1913 in Sandgate Cemetery (ref: Por: ANGLICAN_1 Sec: 18 Lot: 91, Lat: -32.871200545 Long: 151.707377727)

Note 1:
Presumed from the 1861 census (carried out on 7/4/1861) showing his age as 2/12. There is a matching birth registration in Dewsbury (2 miles from Batley), registered in the period Apr-Jun 1861. The Parish baptism record has not been able to be located.
Note that evidence that this is the right Frederick Preston comes from the connection to the younger brother George in the 1881 census. This George can be traced through subsequent censuses to his marriage in 1901 and then his daughter Gladys, who then married Maurice Messenger. Fred’s family in Australia stayed in contact with Gladys and Maurice.

Note 3:
Concluded from the census records showing the birth location of siblings

Note 3:
This birth location might be wrong, as the subsequent 1881 census shows him being born in Batley

Note 4:
Evidence that this is the correct record: the 1881 UK census shows him still in Batley. His daughter Margaret Anne was born in Newcastle Australia in 1885. He must therefore have emigrated sometime between 1881 and 1885. The only Fred Preston to have arrived in this period of the right age arrived on board the S.S. Abergeldie – and this person is shown as coming from Yorkshire.

Note 5:
I have been unable to find any record of the marriage except a reference to it in his wife’s obituary, though the immigration record implies he came to the colony single in Feb 1884, and his first child was born in Newcastle in 1885.
