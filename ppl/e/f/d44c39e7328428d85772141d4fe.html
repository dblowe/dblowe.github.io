Narrative

Story

Connecting Context:
Marriage certificate of Guido Weber

Eleanor (Nell) Talbot was born on 6th January 1877 in Mackay, Queensland, to Richard Hays Talbot (a bootmaker) and Anna Maria Smith. Richard and Anna had separately immigrated to Australia 15 years earlier in 1862 and then married a year later in 1863. They initially moved to Charters Towers where their first 6 children were born, but then by the mid 1870's Richard and Anna had moved to Mackay, where Eleanor was born as the 7th of 8 children (5 girls and 3 boys).

Eleanor then grew up in Mackay attending the Mackay State School. A newspaper article from November 1892 indicates that Eleanor (and another girl from the same school) has passed the Sydney Junior University Examinations. Another article in December 1892 (when Eleanor was nearly 16) describes the end of the school year at the Girls State School, Mackay, and the awarding of prizes including, for Eleanor, a second prize in general proficiency and 1st prize in original composition.

Three and half years later, on 11/6/1896 (when Eleanor was 19) she began work as a teacher. Her employment record indicates she had no professional training and no previous employment. Her first appointment was at Scrubby Mt Provisional School, 30km south of Mackay, and which had only opened 3 months earlier [Note 1]. Upon her first formal evaluation 5 months later, the record states: "She shows exceptional aptitude for teaching; intelligent and energetic … is doing excellent work… she is a smart clever girl".

Eleanor remained at Scrubby Mt for around 3 years, and then on 1/5/1899 she moved a long way south to Glenlyon State School. She was the first teacher in this new school, located in Qld, just across the NSW border. During the next year she met her future husband, Guido Weber. In October 1899 Guido arrived as a teacher at Hawthorne Provisional School, in Hawthorne Parish, Arrawatta - around 10km south of the Queensland border, and 80km from Glenlyon. Guido remained at Hawthorne for 16 months, and on 13th Feb 1901 Guido was moved back south to Marrickville Public School in Sydney, and then 5 months later at the end of July he was the teacher at new school opened at "St Heliers, Sandy Creek" (near Muswellbrook).

It is possible that during Eleanor's time at Glenlyon she spent some time at another school: a newspaper article from 1940 describes the golden jubilee of Ropeley State School (which is 400 km NE from Glenlyon), and lists Eleanor Talbot as the head teacher for 8 months from 1/1/1901 to 31/8/1901 [Note 2].

Guido must have remained in contact with Eleanor Talbot, as the following year on 31/5/1902, after three years at Glenlyon, Eleanor resigned as a teacher. A month later, on 4th July 1902 Eleanor arrived in Sydney from Brisbane aboard the ship Tyrian. Five days later, on 9th July 1902 Eleanor (aged 25) and Guido Weber (aged 23) were married at St Michael's Church in Wollongong [Note 3]. Shortly after the marriage in 1902 Guido and Eleanor moved to Copeland, with Guido teaching at Copeland North Public School (10km west of Gloucester). Over the next few years Guido's salary increased from £88 to £120 per annum.

Guido and Eleanor remained in Copeland for somewhere between 4 and 8 years, becoming part of the local community (Guido became the president of the Copeland School of Arts for 1905 and 1906). Their first two children were born in Copeland - Mona (1903) and Eric (1906). Their third child, Elizabeth (1908), was born in Wollongong - though this might have been during a move. At some point between 1906 and 1910, Guido and Eleanor moved to Lostock, a small town near Dungog.

On 4/4/1911, at the age of only 34, Eleanor died [Note 4]. She was buried in Lostock Anglican cemetery.

Eleven months later, on 1/3/1912, Guido remarried - to Eleanor's younger sister Caroline (now aged 32). The marriage occurred in Balmain, Sydney, and Caroline is listed as a nurse living in Mackay, Queensland, and Guido is listed as living at Speers Point, Boolaroo (south of Newcastle). In 1913 Guido and Caroline, along with the children from the marriage to Eleanor (now aged 5-10) moved to Dubbo, with Guido teaching at Delroy Public School. Guido went on to serve in WW1, being injured at Passchendaele, and have two more children with Caroline. After the war Guido returned to teaching, and the family moved initially back to Wollongong, then Newcastle (where Guido and Eleanor's first child Mona became a teacher and then married George Preston), and then to Tomerong near Nowra. Guido eventually retired to Sylvania, and then finally to Currarong (on the South Coast). Guido died in Ronoli Private Hospital, Wollongong on 25th November 1954, at the age of 75 years - 43 years after Eleanor.

Note 1:
From http://www.archivessearch.qld.gov.au/Search/AgencyDetails.aspx?AgencyId=5988: "Scrubby Mount Provisional School opened 3 February 1896 and became Scrubby Mount State School in 1909. The name was changed to Sunnyside State School in 1911 and the school closed in December 1960".

Note 2:
The Queensland teacher records only list one Eleanor Talbot, so it is likely this is the same Eleanor. Whilst strangely there is no mention of this on Eleanor's teaching record, her record does have a gap from 15/5/00 (when the final evaluation is recorded) to her resignation in 1902.

Note 3:
It is likely Eleanor had no family with her, as the witnesses on the marriage certificate were both Webers, and a newspaper article states "The bride was attended by the Misses Weber, sisters of the bridegroom".

Note 4:
Notes from a relative - Daphne Salt - indicates that Eleanor died in childbirth (with the child also not surviving), though this has not been able to be validated.
