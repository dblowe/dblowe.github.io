Narrative

Story

Note: On the birth index and the marriage record, Bridget’s surname is spelt “Roche”. On some later documents (e.g. birth records for her children, and references to her siblings, the surname is sometimes spelt “Roach”.

Bridget Roche was born in 1846 or 1847 [Note 1] to Martin Roche and Mary ?Carton. She was born on the farm of her father, John Roche, in the Strathbogie Ranges where the town of Yea now stands.

On the 23rd February 1968, aged 20, Bridget married Michael Maher at Hughes Creek. Michael is listed as a farmer living at Tabilk, whilst Bridget is listed as having been born at Yea and currently also living at Tabilk (around 60km north of Yea).

Michael and Bridget subsequently had at least 12 children. Whilst two died as infants, the rest of the children – 5 sons and 5 daughters – all outlived Bridget. The first 7 were born in Avanel/Nagambie (Tabilk, Nagambie, Avanel and Hughes Creek are all within 25km, and around 120km north of Melbourne): Margaret (1869), John (1870), Mary (1872), Martin (1874), Louis (1876), Thomas (1877) and William (1880). The next child – Catherine (1882) – was born in Tatura, 50 km north of Nagambie. The family then moved another 50km north to Numurkah, where the last 4 children were born: Ellen (1884), Annie (1887), Isidore (1889) and Irene May (1891).

In 1882 Bridget’s brother William was a drover. Just 6 months after marrying he drowned crossing the Avoca River at East Charlton whilst driving sheep from the Murrumbidgee to South Australia.

At some point between 1891 and 1904 [Note 2] the family moved another 70km north to Mt Gwynne, just across the border into NSW.

Bridget’s husband, Michael, died on 28th Apr 1919 in Piper Street [Note 3], Yarrawonga. Bridget’s subsequent obituary, 8 years later, stated:

"After Mr. Maher's death, about eight years ago, his widow went to Yarrawonga, and later to Wagga, and lived with her daughters, Mrs. James Hanrahan [i.e. Mary] and Mrs W. Lowe [i.e. Irene May]. Mrs. Maher reared a family of five son's and five daughters, all of whom are living. The sons were present at the funeral. About 50 grandchildren and 20 great-grandchildren are included in those who mourn their loss. Three of deceased's sons are at Yarrawonga, one at Daysdale, and one (Bill) is among the soldier settlers at Griffith, N.S.W."

Bridget died at the home of her daughter Irene May Lowe in Wagga Wagga, NSW, on Saturday 27th Feb 1826. On Sunday 28th February her remains were taken in a long cortege to Yarrawonga, initially to Sacred Heart Chapel and then to the gravesite.

Note 1:
Numerous family trees on Ancestry list the date of birth as 31/7/1846, but no record of this can be found. The birth index indicates the birth was registered in 1847. It would need to be checked against a birth record.

Note 2:
A newspaper article from Dec 1904 lists them living in Mt Gwynne, but attending a wedding at Numurkah.

Note 3:
In later electoral rolls, Bridget and Michael’s son’s Isidore Maher and Thomas Frances Maher (and their wives) are both shown living in Piper Street – though no street addresses are given, so it is no known if they are the same address, and whether this matches where Michael was living when he died.
