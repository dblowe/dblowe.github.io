Narrative

Story

Connecting Context:
The birth certificate of Guido Weber (5-4-1879) gives his mother as Elizabeth Duke Weber, formerly Rice, aged 23 (so, b. ~1855/6), Shoalhaven.

Elizabeth Duke Rice was born on 4th March 1856 at Broughton Creek (now named Berry), Shoalhaven, NSW - the first of four daughters born to William Rice and Honoria Prendergast (Elizabeth, Mysie, Clara and Letitia). William Rice was a Sea Captain and hence was often away at sea whilst Elizabeth was growing up.

At some point the family moved to Darkes Forest, north of Wollongong [Note 1]. In 1874 the 22 year old Carl Weber, son of Prussian immigrants, was working on the initial surveys of the Sydney Water Supply and was camped in a scrubby paddock owned by Mrs Rice at Darkes Forest. Just before Christmas 1874, his health failed and he was nursed back to health by Mrs Rice and Elizabeth.

By the time that Carl was well again, he had asked Mrs Rice for her daughter’s hand in marriage, and had written to Captain Rice on the same subject. They were then married on 13th Jan 1875 at St Marks Church, Appin when Elizabeth was 19 years old. The marriage record describes them both as living at “Dark’s Forest” at the time. Carl later wrote about his married life: "Life was now one holiday, though I was at the bridge [over the Moruya River] almost ten hours out of twelve, the pleasure of having my darling to see me start and to meet me on my return to dinner and in the evening made the days pass quickly."

Over the next 16 years Elizabeth and Carl had 8 children: Guido (1879), Duke (1876), Arnold (1877), Gertrude (1881), Dora (1882), William (1884), Lillian (1887) and Berta (1891), with all of them living to adulthood except Duke. The family appears to have moved around initially (due to Carl's work as a Surveyor) - initially to Clarendon, then Glenn Innes, and then Woonona in Wollongong.

The family then remained in Wollongong until Elizabeth died at the age of 47 on 11th April 1903. An obituary described the circumstances as follows:
"The sad news of the sudden death of Mrs. Weber, wife of Mr. Carl Weber, Surveyor, of Smith Street, on Saturday might last, caused quite a shock throughout Wollongong on becoming known. She had been somewhat unwell during the last three months, but only on Tuesday week last did her illness give special anxiety to her family. And even up to the moment of her death no fear of such an immediate result was entertained. On Saturday evening, however, symptoms being not as desired, Dr. Leo, who had been attending to her throughout, was called in, and during his visit at 9 she suddenly passed away by heart failure. The deceased lasy was most exemplary in the best attributes of woman. As a wife and a mother she was devoted and true in the full sense of the expression…".

She was then buried (on the request of her parents who were both still alive) on 13th April at the Church of England cemetery, Sutherland (now named Woronora cemetery). Her husband Carl lived another 20 years, dying in 1923.

Elizabeth is buried in a family plot with her husband Carl Weber, parents William and Honoria Rice, uncle Samuel Rice, children Berta and Gertrude Weber and Dora Watson, grandchild Eric Weber (son of Guido), and her sisters Letitia, Clara and Mysie Rice

Note 1:
Darkes Forest is a locality about 3km WNW of Flat Rock Junction. During the late 1800's around 20-30 families were living in the area. See http://www.historichelensburgh.org.au/farming-helensburgh-region and http://www.wollongong.nsw.gov.au/library/onlineresources/suburbprofiles/pages/darkesforest.aspx
