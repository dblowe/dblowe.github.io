Narrative

Story

Elizabeth Mary Clark was born in Hope Inn (owned by her father), Ovens River [Note 1] on 30-6-1843, and was christened on 28-1-1846 in St James C/E, Melbourne [Note 2]. She was the third of at least 8 children (though the second oldest to survive beyond infancy) of William Henry Clark and Elizabeth Harris - the earliest settlers in Wangaratta, with William known as the "Father of Wangaratta".

Elizabeth appears to have spent her entire life living at or near Wangaratta (apart from trips to Melbourne) and there is little information available about her life.

On 1st Feb 1864 Elizabeth, aged 20, married John Moore (b. 1843) in Holy Trinity church, Wangaratta. John was the daughter of John Moore snr, an immigrant from Kent in the UK who arrived in NSW in 1838, and Margaret Considine, an Irish immigrant who arrived in 1836. It is interesting to note that 8 years later Elizabeth's younger sister Alice married John's younger brother William.

John and Elizabeth then had 10 children: Charlotte (25 Apr 1864, less than 3 months after the marriage); John George (1866); Margaret (1868 - married John Charles Lowe); Sarah Ann (1870); William Clark (1873); Henry Arthur (1875); Thomas Edward (1877); Elizabeth Mary (1879); Charles Ernest (1881); and Edith Sophia (1884).

In Feb 1877 John applied to bring a section of land in Wangandary [Note 3] under the land statute. In March 1877, Elizabeth applied to bring another section [Note 4] under the land statute. It is unclear, but these sections appear to refer to land being leased by William Clark, so may have been done in connection with him, or as an attempt to bring this land to Auction?

At some point John and Elizabeth appear to have moved from Wangaratta to a nearby farm at Wangandary. Their children up until Thomas (1876) are listed as being born in Wangaratta, but the latter three from Elizabeth (1878) are listed as being born at Wangandary. [Note 5].

Elizabeth died on 19th December 1898, aged 55yo, (in Carlton Women's Hospital, Melbourne) and was buried in Wangaratta Cemetery on 28th December 1898, and John died half a year later on 4th July 1899 in Wangaratta, aged 56yo. Both were buried in Wangaratta.

---

Note 1:
Ovens River was first settled in 1838, just 3 years after Melbourne was settled, by Thomas Rattray. John Clark arrived with his wife the following year in 1839 and bought the "hotel" (a slab bark hut) and punt across the river from Rattray. Whilst initially known as Ovens River, it was renamed Wangaratta in 1848.

Note 2:
The foundation stone for St James was laid in 1839, and it was opened in 1842 and then completed in 1847, so Elizabeth's baptism would have been whilst it was still being built.

Note 3:
The land is described as: "Part of Crown pre-emptive Section A, known as Wangandary, in the Parish of South Wangaratta, County of Moira, commencing at the intersection of the Wangaratta and Bundalong Road and the Ovens River; then south by that road 6020 links; thence east 3140 links; then north 1650 links to a swamp; thence northerly by that swamp to the Ovens River; and then Westerly by that River to the commencing point".

Note 4:
The land is described as: "Part of Crown section A, known as Wangandary, Parish of South Wangaratta, county of Moira, commencing on the western margin of a lagoon; 3140 links east, from a point 4370 links south from the north-west corner of the said section; thence northerly by that margin to the Ovens River; thence south-easterly by that river; thence west 4420 links, and thence north 384 links to the commencing point".

Note 5:
The Parish Plans show an area owned by John Moore (Snr or Jnr?). This is now the area bounded by College St., Appin St., Phillipson St. and Williams Rd.
