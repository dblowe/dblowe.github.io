Narrative

Story

Sarah Gray was born on 19th Jan 1835 in Batley, Yorkshire, to William Gray (a farmer) and Margaret Gray nee Hepworth [Note 1]. She was the fifth child of nine. (Her oldest sibling, John, was born in 1827. Her youngest sibling was born in 1854).

Her family lived in Upper Batley as farmers [Note 2]. In the 1841 census she was living in Upper Batley as a 6 year old with her parents and 3 older and 2 younger siblings. By the age of 16 (in 1851) she had however moved out and was working as the sole house servant for Robert and Anne Clapham (aged 64 and 55) in Brookroyd House (about a mile away) [Note 3].

In Jan 1854, having just turned 19, she had her first child, William, though no father (or different surname) is listed on the Parish record [Note 4]. William appears on the 1861 census living with Sarah and her parents, and in the 1871 census (as a 17 year old) still living with his grandfather (though listed as his son, not grandson) rather than with his mother Sarah and her family.

On Jan 24th 1859, at age 24, she married William Preston (a Watchman from Accrington in Lancashire, who had been transferred to Dewsbury in Oct 1857) [Note 5].

The first son (John) was born in Batley in early 1860, and William was then transferred (location unclear). On 20 Jan 1861 William left the Constabulary due to ill health and returned to Batley. Frederick was then born in Jan/Feb 1861 in Batley. At the time of the 1861 census (April 7th) Sarah was living (presumably temporarily) temporarily with her parents and two young children and William was boarding nearby (with the Spedding family) [Note 6].

In June 1863 William rejoined the constabulary and worked for them for the next 14 years. During this time Sarah and William moved constantly due to William's work: they started in Saddleworth. On 1 Oct 1866 he was moved to W. Morley, and then on 1st Nov 1871 he was transferred to Upper Agbrigg - a district to the SE of Huddersfield.

During this time they had 5 more children: Emma (1865, Saddleworth), Anna (1867, Sowerby Bridge), Arther (1871, Hollywell Green, Stainland [Note 7]), Margaret (1873, Colnebridge), Mary E (1876, Highburton).

In the 1871 census they are shown living in Lower Green, Stainland with children John (11), Fred (9), Ann (4) and Arthur (2 months) (6 year old Emma can’t be found anywhere in the 1871 census, but does appear in the 1881 census).

Sarah’s husband William retired again in 1877 due to ill health and died shortly afterwards. Sarah returned back to Batley, and the last child, George, was born in Batley: (George is the father of Gladys Messenger, who remained in contact with Fred’s descendents in Australia). It is also interesting to note that all the children except John were baptised at the same time (17/11/1877) - 7 months after William died [Note 8].

Sarah then remained in Batley for the rest of her life. The 1881 census shows her in Upper Batley with all of the children except John, the oldest: Fred (20), Emma (16), Ann (14), Arthur (10), Margaret (8), Mary E. (5) and George (3). The 1891 census shows her still in Batley with Emma (26), Annie (24), Mary E. (15) and George (13).

She is likely to have died in early 1900 [Note 9].

Note 1:
The baptism record does not include her mothers name, but Margaret’s name can be found from the Marriage record, which lists her as Margaret Hepworth.

Note 2:
The 1841 census shows her father as a “Farmer”. There is further detail in the 1861 census, which shows her Father as a “Milk Dealer farms 5½ acres”.

Note 3:
The 1851 census shows up as “Sarah Grey” – but no other relevant record can be found for “Sarah Gray” in the 1851 census, so this is likely to be her.

Note 4:
It is quite likely that this is the correct relationship, given the subsequent appearance in relevant census records.

Note 5:
A parish record for this marriage cannot be found. It may be that the marriage was problematic because of the earlier child, or possibly in a non-conformist church? Other hints at this possibility include that all the children (except William and John) were baptized together after her husband William died around 20 years later. See [Web: Robert Fitzakerley] for a reference with a specific date, but no source.

Note 6:
William had recently left the constabulary due to ill health, and so may have been convalescing.

Note 7:
There is a difference in the birth location of Arthur Preston between the 1871 (Stainland) and 1881 (Holleywell Green) censuses – though the two locations are less than a mile apart.

Note 8:
See records for William.

Note 9:
There is only one relevant death index record, and there is no record in the 1901 census.
