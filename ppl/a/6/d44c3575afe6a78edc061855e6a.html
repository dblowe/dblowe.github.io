Story

Connecting Context:
Personal stories (from daughter Joan Preston)

George Arthur Preston was born in Newcastle in 1893 [note 1], the 5th of 10 children to Frederick Preston and Catherine (Kate) Cooley [note 2] (though the third to survive past infancy). He lived around Nobby's Head for the first 20+ years of his life. Around 1910 he started work for Goldsmith's Ltd (a well-known shoe store in Newcastle) where, apart from a break during the war, he remained working until his death.

In 1913 his father died unexpectedly, and then on 15/5/1916 George enlisted for WWI. His enlistment papers give his age as 23 yrs, 3 months, and describes him as single, a show salesman, and his next of kin as his mother, Katherine Preston of Shortland Park. He was initially in "B" Company, Newcastle Depot, with a rank of private. On 4/7/1916 he was transferred to the 2nd Heavy Trench Mortar Battery, Menangle with a rank of gunner.

On 30/9/1916 he embarked at Sydney aboard Aeneas, and then disembarked at Plymouth around 6 weeks later on 19/11/16, and was transferred to Camp Parkhouse (Engineering company, Salisbury plains). On 31/12/1916 he was marched out to Larkhill where he was eventually appointed as a "Driver". On 21/4/1817 he proceeded overseas from Folkestone to Etaples, France, and was then marched out to join the 3rd Australian Field Artillery Brigade (3FAB). The 3FAB then fought at Pozieres, advanced to the Hindenburg Line, Bullecourt, Menin Road, Broodeseinde, Passchendaele, Hazebrouck, and Amiens.

In late 1917 the 3FAB was participating in the 3rd Battle of Ypres, and in October/November, as part of this, the 2nd Battle of Passchendaele. On 2/11/17 George was "injured accidentally", and then on 5/11 was admitted to the 3rd Australian General Hospital at Abbeville with a fractured tibia and fibia. On 14/11/17 he was invalided back to England and admitted to Storton War Hospital. On 5/4/1918 he left England, arriving back in Australia on 1/6/1918 aboard HMAT Borda. A month later, on 4/7/1918 he was discharged due to medical unfitness, having been in the army for just over 2 years and having spent six and a half months on the Western Front.

On 2/5/1925, at the age of 32, he was married in Newcastle to Mona Weber, eldest daughter of Guido Weber of Narrabarba (on the far south coast of NSW). At some point in the next few years (likely around 1928 [Note 3]) George and Mona moved to 10 King Street, Stockton. They then had three children: Douglas (26/7/1926), Maxwell (17/9/1930), and Joan (12/10/1934). George became a well-respected member of the local community - particularly with regard to fishing and the surf clubs.

George died on Saturday 27/11/1948 aged 55 (though his obituary indicated he was in his 54th year [Note 1]). He is buried in Stockton Cemetery. His obituary stated:

"MR. GEORGE A. PRESTON
Mr. George Arthur Preston, of King-street, Stockton, who was in his 54th year when he died last Saturday night, was for 40 years connected with the boot and shoe trade in Newcastle and was an out standing sporting personality.
Mr. Preston was a son of the late Mr. Frederick and Mrs. Kathleen Preston, a well-known Newcastle family, and was for 38 years a member of the staff of Goldsmith's Ltd.
He was a foundation member of Newcastle Surf Club, and retained his membership for 20 years till he moved to Stockton about 15 years ago. He helped form the second Stockton Surf Club and was its first boat captain, being skipper of the first Towns' built surf-boat to be used in the district.
Mr. Preston, a noted beach fisherman, was secretary of the Stock ton branch of the Returned Service men's League for some time, and was an active member till the time of his death. He was a member of the A.I.F. in the first World War.
Mr. Preston, with Mr. R. K. Robertson, brought the first surf canoe to Newcastle. The craft, well known on Newcastle beaches, was the "Stickybeak" and its performances in rough seas were noted. The canoe was used in rescues and exhibition work before surf-boats or surf-skis became popular.
Mr. Preston is survived by his widow and his children, Douglas, Maxwell and Joan. His brother, Mr. John L. Preston, is well known in Newcastle's commercial and sporting life. Other members of the family are a brother, Mr. Howard J. Preston, and sisters, Mesdames C. Hornby, A. Frew and E. Barnes."

Note 1:
There is some uncertainly about the year of birth. The birth index gives the birth as being 1893, and the enlistment documents from 15/5/1916 give his age as 23 yrs, 3 months, which implies he was born around Feb 1893. Conversely, George's obituary from Dec 1948 indicate he was in his 54th year, putting his birth year as 1895.

Note 2:
George's mother's name is unclear. The birth record give his mother as "Kate". George's enlistment document give his next of kin as his mother, Katherine Preston. George's obituary give his mother as Kathleen Preston. Her own obituary, death record, and will all give her name as Catherine Preston. Immigration records give her name as "Kate Cooley".

Note 3:
George's obituary (1948) indicates that "He was a foundation member of Newcastle Surf Club, and retained his membership for 20 years till he moved to Stockton about 15 years ago". Newcastle Surf Club was founded in 1908. Places the move to Stockton between 1928 and 1933. A newspaper article from 1930 however indicated he was elected "Club Captain" of Stockton Surf Club in 1930, so the move to Stockton was prior to 1930.
