Narrative

Story

Connecting Context:
Maria Ann Smith is listed on the marriage certificate with Richard Talbot, and as Ann Maria Smith on the birth record of Eleanor Talbot.

 

Maria Ann Smith was born on 13th May 1843 in Isleworth, Middlesex, England, to William Smith and Maria Constance (Constant) Paine. She was then baptised on 9th June 1843 at All Saints, Isleworth. Her father William was born ~1813 in Isleworth and her mother Maria was baptised on 1st March 1811 in Ickenham, 11 miles away. They were then (possibly) married in Q3 1842 in St George, Hanover Sq. [Note 1].

Maria was the oldest of 8 children, though only four of these children appear to have survived past childhood: Maria Ann (1843), William Edward (1844-), Thomas (1845-1850), Walter Richard (1846-1847), Michael (1848-1848), Walter Henry (1849-), Elizabeth (1850-?1850), George (~1854-). All of these children were born in Isleworth, England.

By 1851, the UK Census lists Maria living at 80 Church Street, Isleworth with her father William (38yo, Ironmonger), mother Maria C (39yo), siblings William Edward (6) and Walter Henry (2), and a house servant Ada Ayres (17yo).

By 1861, it is likely her father has died [Note 2], and the 1861 Census lists Maria as 17yo, an "Assistant Ironmonger", and living in ?Phenis Row with her mother Maria (50, Ironmonger, b. Ickenham), siblings William (16, Ironmonger), Walter (12, scholar), George R (7, scholar), and Edward Smith (visitor, 76yo, P??? maker), Elizabeth Laurance (41, Boarder), Hannah Swift (18, House servant).

Whilst her mother remained in Isleworth until her death 30 years later, Maria migrated to Australia in 1862 as an 18 year old aboard the "City of Brisbane" [Note 3]. The same ship also included the Talbot Family. It is not known whether Maria knew the Talbots before leaving England, but they were from nearby Hammersmith (about 8 miles from Isleworth), and Elizabeth Talbot (nee Walton) grew up in Isleworth (1820's and early 1830's - before Maria was born), so it is possible.

On 14/7/1863, around 1 year after arriving in Qld, Maria married Richard Talbot in Rockhampton. The marriage certificate shows them both living in Yaamba, with Richard a shoemaker and Maria a milliner. Their ages are not given. The same immigration and subsequent marriage suggests that Maria might have stayed with the Talbot family after their arrival in Australia. Richard's records indicate that after arriving he "proceeded to Yamala, on the Peak Downs, where he remained for 12 months. He removed thence to Yaamba".

Richard and Maria then are likely to have remained in Yaamba until 1869 and then moved to Mackay (see Richard's story for more information), where Richard worked as a bootmaker, and where they stayed for the next 48 years. In the 16 years following their marriage they had 8 children: Louisa (31/3/1864), Edward (14/12/1865), Richard (2/7/1867), Charles (22/8/1869) [Note 4], Isabella (7/9/1871), Elizabeth (25/9/1873), Eleanor (1877) and Caroline (10/7/1879).

An 1875 newspaper item indicates that Richard Talbot, bootmaker, Mackay, was offering a Â£1 reward for a lost mare. Two years later in 1877, a newspaper article lists Richard as a member of the School Committee of the State School at Mackay.

In 1878, Richard applied to take over the licence for the Steam Packet Hotel in Mackay. He took over this hotel for a relatively short period from 1879 to 1880. On St Patricks day 1879, a poem published in the local Newspaper refers to Richard: "There's sturdy R. H. Talbot, you can see his duster wave". A few months later, another newspaper entry refers to the birth of Maria and Richard's last daughter, Caroline, born at the Steam Packet Hotel.

By July 1882, things appear to have deteriorated for Richard and Maria, and Richardwas declared insolvent. By November the same year, Richard was advertising for work: "R.H. Talbot, Bookmaker, is to be found Next to the River View Hotel. All orders punctually executed either in making or repairs". He may well have rebuilt over the next decade, as there is an article from 1891 indicating that "there passed on 25th [July] seventy-five bullocks from Redcliffe to Rockhampton, the property of R.H. Talbot". In 1905, he is described as one of 7 shareholders in the North Queensland Meat Export Co., Ltd.

On 11/9/1917 (when Maria was 74 and Richard was 77) a newspaper article stated "Mr. and Mrs R.H. Talbot, who have been residents of Mackey for 48 years past, intend leaving by the S.S. Bingera on Tuesday for Sydney, where they will in future reside". Richard then died around 6 years later, on 8/7/1923 at the age of 83, at Sacred Heart Hospice, Darlinghurst. He is buried at Rookwood Cemetery (section 7, row 20, grave 819).

Maria then lived another 5 years, dying in Brisbane, on 28/7/1928 at the age of 85. She was buried in Bulimba Cemetery

Note 1:
A definitive marriage record for William and Maria has not been found, though there is a possible record for Maria Constant Paine and William Smith in St George Hanover Square in 1842. This would however mean that Maria was ~31 years old when first married - which would be unusual.

Note 2:
A definitive death record has not yet been located, but there are two possibilities: one has a William Smith being buried in Isleworth on 14-1-1855, and another on 24-1-1857.

Note 3:
There was also a 16yo Charoline Smith on the same ship, though no connection can be found. There was no related marriage of Charoline to a Talbot, and there is no connection to a Charoline (or Caroline) Smith in Isleworth.

Note 4:
Anecdotal information (from Joan Lowe) indicates that Charles ended up living on a plantation on New Guinea.
